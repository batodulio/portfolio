Visual identity system

Bonding made simple.

We're all about genuine connection and fun. A bold, colorful, game-show-bright identity system built for icebreaker games, corporate events, classrooms, and gatherings of every kind.

01 — Color system
Primary & accent palette

IceBrk Blue, Energy Orange, Sky Blue, and Vitality Green form the signature quartet — bold, saturated, and built to feel like a game show, not a dashboard. Sunshine Yellow and Playful Purple step in for celebratory moments: badges, timers, and team colors. Use at least two or three of the four primaries together in any hero section or key visual.

02 — Decorative shape language
Confetti blobs & bold bursts

Where a corporate system uses thin dotted rings at low opacity, IceBrk's shape language is loud on purpose. Solid color blobs bleed off card and section edges like confetti. Scattered dots and triangles sit in mixed primary colors near celebratory moments. Everything stays at full or near-full opacity — if a shape doesn't read as fun at a glance, it's too subtle for this brand.

Confetti blob — on white
Solid filled circles at full opacity, bleeding off the card corner like a balloon.
Scattered dots
Small mixed-color dots near CTAs and celebratory headlines — never near body text.
Confetti blob — on blue
Sky Blue and Sunshine Yellow at full opacity create depth on hero sections.
Confetti blob — on orange
Pair any primary with one contrasting blob plus a small white accent dot.
Triangle accents
Small bold triangles add party-confetti energy alongside the dots and blobs.
Layered blobs
Two overlapping blobs of different sizes and colors read as more dynamic than one.
Shape usage quick reference
Placement
  • Corner-bleed (most common)
  • Behind headlines / hero text
  • Around winning/celebration moments
  • Scattered near CTAs
Sizing guide
  • Hero/page: 200–340px
  • Card sections: 60–160px
  • Confetti dots: 8–18px
Opacity rules
  • Blobs: 80–100%
  • Dots / triangles: 90–100%
  • Never below 70% — IceBrk is bold, not subtle

  border-radius: 50%;
  pointer-events: none;

05 — Brand voice
How IceBrk talks

Casual, warm, and emotion-first — like a fun friend hosting the party, never a vendor pitching software. Positive scripting only: say what IceBrk does, not what it removes.

"Create instant bonding and memories with friends, zero setup needed!"

06 — Brand rules
Do's & don'ts
✓ Do
  • Use IceBrk Blue, Energy Orange, Sky Blue, and Vitality Green together as the signature combo
  • Keep type bold and rounded — Baloo 2 for display, Poppins for body
  • Use full-opacity confetti blobs, dots, and triangles
  • Keep corners rounded everywhere — buttons, cards, containers
  • Lead messaging with fun, connection, and memories
  • Use positive scripting — say what IceBrk does, not what it removes
✕ Don't
  • Don't default to a single muted corporate color as the dominant anchor
  • Don't use serif fonts or thin/light font weights
  • Don't use low-opacity (6–15%) decorative shapes — this brand is bold, not subtle
  • Don't use sharp corners or thin hairline borders
  • Don't write headlines using negation words (no, zero, less, can't)
  • Don't let copy sound corporate or vendor-like
07 — Accent strategy
When to use each color
Vitality green

Correct answers, completed games, success badges, and any CTA signaling positive forward motion — "Start game," "Join the fun."

Energy orange

Primary CTAs, "Customize" actions, high-energy moments, and anything that needs to grab attention without feeling alarming.

Sunshine yellow

Timers, countdowns, urgency states, and celebratory badges. Pairs with Charcoal Ink text, never white.

Playful purple

Team colors and alternate category tags — use to add variety when more than four distinct colors are needed.
